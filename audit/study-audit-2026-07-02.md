## 1. Verdict

The pipeline is now in materially good shape. The crawl is genuinely complete for
depth 2, every headline number in the manuscript reconciles exactly with the
DuckDB store and the output tables, seed de-duplication is correct, provenance is
recorded, and the package builds and passes its tests. This is a real,
internally consistent 11,510,983-node / 31,563,370-edge graph. No numerical
correctness bug was found.

It is **not yet publication-ready for a high-impact research-integrity journal**,
for three reasons:

1. **Reproducibility (C1).** The manuscript and all six manuscript figures are
   not generated by any committed code. The primary scientific artifact cannot be
   regenerated from source.
2. **A misleading headline number (R1).** "Post-notice depth-2 descendants =
   17,727,422" is a per-seed sum with multiplicity that exceeds the total number
   of depth-2 nodes (10,659,409) and is presented as if it were a distinct-paper
   count.
3. **Under-substantiated framing (M1).** The causal-sounding "pollution" claim
   rests on descriptive counts with no baseline, comparator, or citation-context
   classification (disclosed in Limitations, but a top venue will push on it).

Everything else is moderate/minor and enumerated below.

## 3. Findings — Methodology

### M1 (Major, disclosed). No baseline / comparator for a causal-sounding claim
The title and framing ("pollution") imply harm propagating through the
literature, but every metric is a descriptive count. There is no pre/post
citation-rate model, no matched non-retracted control, no time-at-risk
denominator, and no citation-context (approving vs correcting) classification.
The manuscript discloses all of this in Limitations, which is good practice, but
for a top research-integrity journal the "pollution" claim is under-substantiated
without at least a descriptive baseline (e.g. citations-per-year aligned to the
notice date, or expected citations for matched non-retracted papers).
**Decision needed:** either soften the framing to a pure network census, or add a
descriptive comparator.

### M2 (Moderate). Post-notice attribution uses global BFS depth
Per-seed reach is computed on globally-labeled depth: a node's depth is its
shortest distance from ANY seed (`create_large_analysis_temps` requires
`fs.depth = 2` for depth-2 descendants). A paper two hops from seed S but one hop
from seed T is attributed to T at depth 1 and is not counted among S's depth-2
descendants. This is internally consistent with the manuscript's global
definitions and is a legitimate design, but it means `total_depth2_reach` is a
global-graph neighborhood, not an independent per-seed ego-network. Add one
explicit Methods sentence so reviewers do not read it as a per-seed 2-hop count.

### M3 (Moderate). No-DOI seeds silently drop ~9% of the sample
5,912 of 68,249 loaded records (8.9%) lack a resolvable DOI and are excluded from
this OpenCitations-only run (`resolved_status = 'no_doi'`). These skew older /
non-journal / lower-resource — exactly the literature most likely to be
under-corrected. Disclosed as a count, but the likely direction of selection bias
is not stated.

### M4 (Moderate). Overcount parents were not reconciled
Reconciliation re-fetched the 985 UNDERcount parents (bulk found fewer edges than
the citation-count dump) with 0 failures, but left the 9,187 OVERcount parents
as-is (bulk found MORE than the dump). Overcounts can indicate spurious or
duplicate bulk edges and could inflate the edge total. The manuscript reports the
9,187 number but does not explain the mechanism or bound the possible inflation.

### M5 (Minor). No publication-date sanity filtering
12 works carry `publication_year > 2026` (up to 2109) and the minimum is 1473.
Future-dated works are counted as "post-notice." The count is negligible (12 of
11.5M) but signals no date validation; a reviewer may ask.

### M6 (Minor). ~24% of seeds have zero observed reach
14,678 of 60,305 seeds (24.3%) have zero direct citers and zero depth-2 reach in
OpenCitations. The reported median of 12 is correct, but this large zero-mass —
driven by citation-index coverage, not by "clean" papers — is not surfaced and
materially shapes interpretation of the reach distribution.

## 4. Findings — Code

### C1 (Major). The manuscript and its figures are not reproducible
No committed code generates `manuscript.md`, the six `manuscript_figure*` files,
or the `network_density_node_bins.csv` / `network_density_edge_bins.csv` tables.
Confirmed by searching `R/`, `inst/`, `tests/`, and `legacy/`. Only `report.md`
(via `R/report.R`) and three analysis figures (`depth_counts`,
`top_polluted_seeds`, `post_notice_timeline`) are code-generated. The manuscript's
headline numbers (edge-depth split 965,041 / 30,598,329; post-notice depth-2
17,727,422) exist only inside hand-made SVG/PNG figures. For a research-integrity
submission this is the single most important gap: the primary artifact must be
regenerable from source.
**Fix:** add a committed manuscript-and-figure generation script (Quarto or
`R/manuscript.R`) that derives every number and figure from the store or the CSVs.

### C2 (Moderate). The DuckDB analysis path that produced the paper is untested
`tests/testthat/test-analysis.R` exercises the in-memory `run_analysis()` /
`seed_metrics()` path. The completed study ran through the >5M-edge branch —
`run_analysis_duckdb()` + `large_seed_metrics()` + `create_large_analysis_temps()`
— so the exact SQL that produced every headline number has no unit test. Add a
fixture test that forces the DuckDB path (`options(retractionpollution.analysis_in_memory_edge_limit = 0)`
or similar) on a toy store and asserts the SQL metrics equal the in-memory
metrics.

### C3 (Minor). Package hygiene / release debt
- `NAMESPACE` uses `exportPattern("^[[:alpha:]]+")`, exporting every internal
  helper (`validation_row`, `db_single_row`, `knitr_table_md`, ...) → the R CMD
  check "undocumented objects" WARNING. Export only the public API and add
  roxygen `@export` tags.
- `DESCRIPTION` describes OpenAlex resolution + a depth-3 capped export, neither
  used in this run; fine as package capability but should not be read as the
  study method.
- R CMD check: 2 WARNINGs — one benign environment note (`igraph`/`duckdb` built
  under a different R minor version) and the undocumented-exports one above. The
  earlier LICENSE-pointer warning is gone.
- The repo is effectively one "Initial commit" plus a large uncommitted working
  tree; the exact code state that produced the outputs is not version-pinned.

### C4 (Verified correct — no action)
Seed canonicalization (`canonical_seed_records`: dedup by `openalex_id`/DOI,
earliest notice date, concatenated notice types), the min-depth edge merge,
`analysis_edge_scope` reconciliation, the streaming GraphML export + manifest,
and the two-hop depth-2 SQL are all correct and reconcile with the store. The
`report.R` double-write bug flagged in a prior audit is fixed (placeholder →
validate → single write).

## 5. Findings — Reporting (text, tables, figures)

### R1 (Major). "Post-notice depth-2 descendants = 17,727,422" is a misleading total
This is the sum over seeds of a per-seed count; a depth-2 paper is counted once
per seed it descends from (verified multiplicity: `sum(depth2_descendants)` =
26,762,768 vs 10,659,409 unique nodes, ~2.5×). The reported 17,727,422 EXCEEDS
the total number of depth-2 nodes (10,659,409), so it cannot be read as distinct
papers — yet the manuscript table ("Total post-notice depth-2 descendants") and
Figure 4 present it as a bare count.
**Fix:** report the unique-node count, or relabel explicitly as "post-notice
seed→descendant relationships (counted once per seed)."

### R2 (Moderate). "Total post-notice direct citers = 329,693" is also a sum
Verified unique post-notice direct-citer nodes = **303,793**; 329,693 is the
per-seed sum (8.5% inflation; avg 1.09 seeds per citer; one hub paper cites 197
distinct seeds). Smaller effect than R1 but the same labeling fix applies. The
abstract sentence "329,693 direct citers were published after the corresponding
notice date" should say unique citers = 303,793, or be relabeled as citation
relationships.

### R3 (Major, user-flagged). Figure 2 (density network) is uninformative
No quantitative y-axis; the depth-2 panel is a near-uniform blue field; edge
direction is not shown; the orange "fan" is an artifact of degree-sort ordering,
not real structure. It restates Figure 3 decoratively. Replace with a
quantitative figure (see §6).

### R4 (Moderate). Figure 4 is a log-scale two-bar chart
A log y-axis on a 2-bar comparison compresses the true 54× gap (17.7M / 329k) to
a small visual difference, defeating the purpose of a bar chart, and it inherits
the R1 multiplicity problem. Replace (see §6).

### R5 (Minor). Manuscript figure file names vs in-text numbers are scrambled
In-text "Figure 3" is file `manuscript_figure5_edge_depths`; "Figure 5" is
`manuscript_figure2_top_seed_reach`; etc. Readers see sequential 1–6 (fine) but
the file↔number mismatch is a maintenance hazard; a regeneration script should
name figures by their in-text number.

### R6 (Verified consistent — no action)
Every other manuscript number matches `summary.csv`, `report.md`, and the DB:
node/edge totals, depth counts, the top-10 seed table, reach quantiles, and
seeds-with-post-notice counts (34,735 direct / 37,274 depth-2). Transcription is
faithful; the issue is semantics (R1/R2), not arithmetic.

## 6. Visualization recommendations

The current network figure (Figure 2) is the weakest artifact and, per the
author, "almost unusable." All replacements should be generated by committed code
from the store/CSVs.

- **Replace Figure 2 (recommended default): a 2-panel quantitative figure** that
  carries the headline "concentrated but large":
  - (a) log-log complementary CDF (survival curve) of seed-level
    `total_depth2_reach`, annotated with median (12), p99 (7,965), max (197,428),
    and the zero-reach mass (24.3%);
  - (b) a Lorenz / concentration curve of downstream reach (top X% of seeds carry
    Y% of total reach) with the Gini coefficient.
  Alternatives considered: rank-size (Zipf) plot; or a properly sampled /
  top-N-seed force-directed network instead of the full hairball.
- **Replace Figure 4:** use the already-computed post-notice timeline (citations
  per year) or an **event-study** view (citations aligned to notice year 0,
  showing the pre/post distribution) — the strongest honest visual for
  "papers keep being cited after notice." Report unique-node post-notice counts,
  not the multiplicity sums.
- **Keep and clean** Figures 1, 3, 5, 6 (depth counts, edge depths, top-seed
  stacked reach, post-notice) with value labels and corrected file names.
- **Optional:** a matched pre/post descriptive panel if M1 is addressed.

## 8. Bottom line

The data-acquisition and analysis layers are sound and the numbers are correct
and internally consistent; the completed depth-2 graph is a genuine, verifiable
